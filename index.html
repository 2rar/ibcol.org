Get Started

International Blockchain Olympiad (IBCOL) is an annual competition inviting current and recent students to propose solutions to real-world problems using blockchain technology.

HKBCOL combines the practicality of a traditional case competitions with the open-ended problem-solving culture of hack-a-thons. As a team of up to 5 students, competitors will submit their case as using an academic conference style white-paper along with a pitch and presentation deck.

  • No registration costs for a new participants!
  • Participation and mentroship is completely free!
  • New experience and certificates are guaranteed!

The mission of the International Blockchain Olympiad (IBCOL) is to support sustainability and maturity in blockchain by working with academic, industry, and government partners. Primarily working with secondary and tertiary students, IBCOL provides education and training on designing complete blockchain solutions. The International Blockchain Olympiad (IBCOL) is an annual global competition inviting current and recent students to propose solutions for real-world problems with blockchain technology. In a worldwide hunt for blockchain talent, IBCOL combines the practicality of case competitions with the open-ended problem-solving culture of hack-a-thons, where participants submit a whitepaper like an academic conference and implementations of capstone projects

Hall of Fame

Hall of Fame

The History of All Previous International Blockchain Olympiads are Here

International Blockchain Olympiad 2017

The IBCOL was founded in 2017 at the height of the cryptocurrency bubble. While most of the world was distracted by ill-conceived ideas or outright scams, the IBCOL was one of the few that dared to ignore short-term gain for long-term development of the blockchain ecosystem.

International Blockchain Olympiad 2018

IBCOL 2018 organised various workshops as Blockchain from Technical First Principles and enhanced blockchain in business applications. Contestants from all HK universities were competing during this event.

International Blockchain Olympiad 2019

The International Blockchain Olympiad (IBCOL) is a multidisciplinary design and building competition for students worldwide, bringing together hungry, creative young minds to solve real-world challenges through decentralised applications.

International Blockchain Olympiad 2020

IBCOL 2020 competition was a significant step in founding future leaders and professionals in Hong Kong blockchain ecosystem. Decentralized applications of many startup ideas are an essential step towards better future of blockchain education.

International Blockchain Olympiad 2021

International Blockchain Olympiad is a multidisciplinary design and building competition. IBCOL 2021 invites students from around the world to solve global problems using blockchain solutions.

International Blockchain Olympiad 2022

We will further develop our community around the world and invite blockchain professionals to develop education and start-up culture in Hong Kong.

Submission Material Specifications

Submission Material Specifications Whitepaper (required for post-secondary; optional for secondary): pdf file, 10-page limit excluding references and appendices. Pitch Deck: pdf and pptx file, no slide limit. Pitch Deck Presentation: mp4 file or YouTube, 10-minute limit. Posterboard (optional): Figma or PDF, landscape, dimensions 36” × 48” at 300 dpi or equivalent (14400×10800 pixels). Posterboard Presentation (optional): mp4 file or YouTube unlisted URL, 10-minute limit. Elevator Pitch (optional): mp4 file or YouTube unlisted URL, 1-minute limit. Prototype (optional): it does not need to be chaincode or smart contracts, but even an interactive mockup to demonstrate some workflows of your solution would be helpful. Prototype Demonstration (optional): mp4 file or YouTube unlisted URL, no limit (but recommended not to exceed 10 minutes)..

Project Eligibility

Project Eligibility One Team, One Project: A team may submit only one project. Note: a team submitting a project for the IBCOL competition may also submit another entry for our sister competition IDSOL. Returning Participation: Previous projects that have not won any awards from prior IBCOL competitions are eligible to compete in IBCOL. Competition Tier: Secondary school students and post-secondary school students shall compete in their respective tracks with different deadlines and submission criteria.
