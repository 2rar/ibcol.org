HKBCOL 2021 Rules

Read me if you are competing in HKBCOL 2021

The International Blockchain Olympiad is an annual competition for all students. Please note that the Hong Kong Blockchain Olympiad has slightly different definitions from the International Blockchain Olympiad and slightly different eligibility for secondary- and tertiary-level contestants. Also know the dates and requirements for submissions.

Eligibility

Individual Eligibility

  1. Students* only: Any current or recent student may register as a competitor; a recent student would have been a current student until 1 year ago as of the beginning of the 2020-2021 Season (5 July 2020 to 4 July 2021) — if a student had stopped school on or after 5 July 2019, the student is eligible to compete, otherwise, the student is ineligible to compete.
  2. No age limit: A current student is defined as a person of any age that is enrolled in a recognized secondary or post-secondary academic institution.
  3. Representation (Secondary Track): An individual is actively enrolled in a secondary school in Hong Kong.
  4. Representation (Post-Secondary Track): An individual may be attached to an academic institution by being ever enrolled in it; a recognized institution is an accredited educational institution or an institution that may grant a degree or diploma; the institution may or may not be in Hong Kong.
  5. Returning Participation: Previous individuals are eligible to compete.

Team Eligibility

  1. Team Composition (Secondary Track): Projects are submitted by teams of one (1) to five (5) eligible individuals; one participant can be part of more than one team; each team shall have a teacher-in-charge, as the main contact person; a teacher-in-charge may be in-charge of more than one team.
  2. Team Composition (Post-Secondary Track): Projects are submitted by teams of one (1) to six (6) eligible individuals; one participant can be part of more than one team; each team shall have a team lead, as the main contact person.
  3. Team Uniqueness: Each team must be unique, i.e. a team may not share more than 50% of the same composition as any other team, round to the lower number if the team is an odd number; team leads must be unique, i.e. one person cannot be the team lead of multiple teams.
  4. Team Representation (Secondary Track): Each team must have students who are all from the same secondary school in Hong Kong.
  5. Team Representation (Post-Secondary Track): Each team must nominate one representative academic institution if any of the team members are from different academic institutions; teams may be composed of members from different countries/territories or academic institutions, but at least one of them must be from Hong Kong.
  6. Returning Participation: Previous teams are eligible to compete.

Project Eligibility

  1. One Team, One Project: A team may submit only one project. Note: a team submitting a project for the IBCOL competition may also submit another entry for our sister competition IDSOL.
  2. Returning Participation: Previous projects that have not won any awards from prior IBCOL competitions are eligible to compete in IBCOL.
  3. Competition Tier: Secondary school students and post-secondary school students shall compete in their respective tracks with different deadlines and submission criteria.

Submissions

Requirements and Deadlines

  1. Initial Submission Requirements (Secondary Track): Each project shall be evaluated by a posterboard submission by 23:59 28 May 2021.
  2. Initial Submission Requirements (Post-Secondary Track): Each project shall be evaluated by a whitepaper submission by 23:59 19 April 2021.
  3. Finalist Submission Requirements (Secondary Track): Each project shall be evaluated by a posterboard presentation, submitted by 17:00 18 June 2021.
  4. Finalist Submission Requirements (Post-Secondary Track): Each project shall be evaluated by the following: Pitch Deck, Pitch Deck Presentation, Posterboard (optional), Posterboard Presentation, Elevator Pitch, Prototype (optional), Prototype Demonstration (optional); additional materials should be submitted by 17:00 18 June 2021.

Material Specifications

  1. Whitepaper (required for post-secondary; optional for secondary): pdf file, 10-page limit excluding references and appendices.
  2. Pitch Deck: pdf and pptx file, no slide limit.
  3. Pitch Deck Presentation: mp4 file or YouTube, 10-minute limit.
  4. Posterboard (optional): Figma or PDF, landscape, dimensions 36” × 48” at 300 dpi or equivalent (14400×10800 pixels).
  5. Posterboard Presentation (optional): mp4 file or YouTube unlisted URL, 10-minute limit.
  6. Elevator Pitch (optional): mp4 file or YouTube unlisted URL, 1-minute limit.
  7. Prototype (optional): it does not need to be chaincode or smart contracts, but even an interactive mockup to demonstrate some workflows of your solution would be helpful.
  8. Prototype Demonstration (optional): mp4 file or YouTube unlisted URL, no limit (but recommended not to exceed 10 minutes).

Participation

Training

  1. Training (Post-Secondary): Post-secondary students are eligible for the training program that is subsidized by HSBC — i.e. free for post-secondary students — and may find out more about those activities through our blockchain training schedule.
  2. Training (Secondary): Secondary school students are eligible for training programs delivered through the ABC5-Lab program that is eligible for subsidy from the IT Innovation Lab programme from the OGCIO, one of the supporting organisations of IBCOL.

Certification

  1. Level 1: Blockchain Fundamentals
  2. Level 2: Blockchain Solution Design
  3. Level 3: Blockchain Development (R3 Corda, Hyperledger Fabric, Algorand)
